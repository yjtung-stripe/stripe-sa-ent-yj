S Spaceship / Alf — Connect Architecture & Fund Flow Reference

Spaceship (by Namecheap) is building Alf — an AI-powered SaaS e-commerce platform where globally distributed sellers create online stores. Spaceship is US-based. Sellers connect their own Stripe accounts via OAuth and process payments directly. Spaceship monetizes via processing fee markup (Buy Rate spread) and per-transaction application fees on top of a $5/mo subscription. The architecture is designed so Spaceship has minimal involvement in payment operations — Stripe manages risk, sellers manage their own refunds and disputes.

Architecture Confirmed Updated Apr 14, 2026 Phase 2 — E-commerce Launch

3   Cross-Border Fee Scenarios

How Geography Affects Fees on a $100 Direct Charge

With Direct Charges, the seller is the Merchant of Record. Cross-border and FX fees depend on the relationship between the buyer's card-issuing country and the seller's settlement country — not the platform's country. Spaceship's US domicile only affects the application fee settlement (FX on the app fee when it lands in USD).

4   Refund Mechanics — $100 Full Refund

⚠
Void vs. Refund: If a charge is voided before settlement (typically within the authorization window, ~24hrs), interchange may be recoverable. After settlement, interchange is permanently kept by the card network. Spaceship should advise sellers to cancel/void quickly when possible rather than refunding days later.
5   Connected Account Lifecycle & Fees

When Platform Fees Trigger — and When They Don't

Platform fees are tied to account activity, not account existence. A connected account that is idle incurs zero fees. The key trigger is a payout — any payout from the connected account's balance to a bank account in a given calendar month.

Subscription cancellation playbook: When a seller cancels their Alf subscription, Spaceship does not need to disconnect the account. If the seller stops taking payouts, all fees stop automatically. The balance remains in the seller's account. If the seller returns months later and reconnects to a new subscription, no initiation fee is re-charged.
⚠
CBSP Correction (Critical): Since June 2021, Standard accounts are governed by "Controlled By Single Platform" policy. A Standard account can only be connected to one platform at a time (with read_write OAuth scope). The prior Q&A doc incorrectly stated balances are "shared across platforms." This is not possible. A seller must disconnect from Platform A (e.g., Wix) before connecting to Spaceship. Pre-existing balances from Wix remain in the seller's account but are the seller's money — not tied to any platform.
6   Buy Rate vs. Rev Share — Economic Comparison

Why Buy Rate Is the Recommended Model

Spaceship's IC+ rate (0.025% + $0.02) is extraordinarily low — among the lowest of any Stripe customer. This creates massive margin opportunity under Buy Rate that Rev Share cannot replicate. Rev Share at $5M Year 1 volume yields ~5 BPS.

Hybrid approach deferred: Running both Buy Rate and Rev Share simultaneously is technically possible but requires two separate charge-creation codepaths, two fee-display logics, and two reconciliation flows. At ~$5M Year 1 volume, the Rev Share leg would generate ~$2,500/year — the engineering cost far exceeds this. Launch with Buy Rate only. Re-evaluate when Spaceship opens a European entity or hits margin compression on specific corridors.
